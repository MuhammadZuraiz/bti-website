British Training Institute · Homepage Redesign

Three visual directions for a livelier BTI.

A pre-build design exploration. Same content, same lead-capture purpose, same catalogue logic — three distinct ways to make the homepage feel lively, navy-led, and conversion-clear. No production code yet: this is the direction we agree on before we build.

Shared foundation

What all three directions hold constant

These are non-negotiables carried across every option, so the choice is about personality and energy — not about re-litigating strategy.

A

Navy is the identity

BTI navy/blue dominates surfaces and structure. Red is reserved strictly for action — never decoration.

B

One lead engine, preserved

The existing admissions form, WhatsApp and phone flow stay intact. We restyle the entry points, not the plumbing.

C

Trust gets promoted

Est. 2002, IELTS centre, Cambridge, ISO move from hidden text to near-hero proof in every direction.

D

Image-resilient by design

Gradient panels, abstract blue/red shapes and graceful fallbacks mean it looks strong even with no perfect photos.

E

Section contrast, not card walls

Alternating light/dark/tinted bands and varied layouts replace the long repeated white card grids.

F

Three-tier CTA model

Primary (red), Secondary (navy/outline), Tertiary (text + WhatsApp). One clear hierarchy kills CTA fatigue.

Motion principle

One orchestrated page-load reveal beats scattered fidgeting. Every direction is built reduced-motion-safe and keeps animation off the critical reading path.

01

Direction one

Bold Energetic Blue/Red

High-energy and conversion-forward. Motion-rich hero, floating course cards, a live course ticker, and unmissable red CTAs aimed squarely at the individual learner ready to act.

Now enrolling
IELTS Prep Business English Accounting HR & Admin
Hero wireframe · desktop

Desktop concept

A near-full-height dark hero anchored by an animated blue gradient mesh with red light-streaks. An oversized headline sits left; a loose cluster of three tilted, glassy course cards drifts on the right. A live "Now enrolling" ticker runs beneath the fold line, immediately followed by count-up stat counters. Sections then alternate dark → light → tinted so the page pulses with rhythm rather than scrolling as one flat sheet.

Mobile concept

Full-bleed dark hero, headline stacked tight, a single red primary CTA. Course cards become a horizontal swipe carousel; the ticker collapses into a scrolling chip row. A persistent bottom action bar keeps "Speak to Admissions" + WhatsApp one thumb-tap away.

Hero layout

Left column: eyebrow → bold headline → one-line promise → red primary + outline secondary → inline trust chips (Est. 2002 · IELTS Centre). Right column: parallax cluster of floating course cards over the mesh. Ticker spans full width at the base as a transition into the body.

Color usage

Navy — base surfaces, dark sections
Electric blue — energy, glow, motion
Red — primary CTA only
Cream/white — relief bands

SecondBrowse Courses
TertiaryWhatsApp

Motion ideas

  • ●Slow-drifting hero gradient mesh
  • ●Parallax float on course cards
  • ●Marquee course ticker
  • ●Count-up stat numbers on view
  • ●Staggered reveal + magnetic CTA hover

Section order

1 · Motion hero → 2 · Course ticker ✦ → 3 · Stat counters → 4 · Course explorer (tabbed) → 5 · Transformation → 6 · Trust band ✦ → 7 · Placement + Corporate → 8 · Admissions journey → 9 · Resources + FAQ → 10 · Final CTA

▲ Better than current

  • Replaces the static text-left hero with depth, motion and energy.
  • Ticker + live counters signal an active, in-demand institute.
  • Alternating dark sections eliminate the white-grid monotony.
  • Strongest direction for individual-learner conversion.

▼ Risks / avoid

  • Motion overload can hurt performance and feel "marketing-y."
  • Dark backgrounds demand careful contrast/accessibility QA.
  • Could read less premium for corporate buyers — keep it disciplined.
  • Hold the line: red stays for action, or the energy turns to noise.
02

Direction two

Premium Corporate Training

Polished and authoritative. Deep navy depth, gold-accented credibility, structured trust panels and restrained motion — built to win corporate decision-makers and signal two decades of standing.

Est. 2002
IELTS Centre Cambridge ISO Certified 20+ Years
Hero wireframe · desktop

Desktop concept

A composed navy hero with layered depth and a fine vertical-line texture — confident, not loud. A single framed image/gradient panel sits right with a floating "Est. 2002" credential chip. Critically, a full accreditation strip (IELTS · Cambridge · ISO · 20+ years) pins directly under the hero, so proof is the first thing read. Below: credibility pillars, structured editorial course cards, and a dedicated dark corporate training feature band given real estate it never had before.

Mobile concept

Navy hero stacks cleanly; accreditation badges become a tidy scrollable row directly beneath. Content flows as well-spaced structured panels rather than a card dump. Motion is limited to soft fades — the restraint is the premium signal.

Hero layout

Left: gold eyebrow → authoritative headline → supporting sentence → red primary + navy secondary → inline micro-credentials. Right: framed proof panel + Est. 2002 chip. Accreditation bar pinned full-width below as the hero's foundation.

  • Can feel corporate-cold for individual learners and parents.
  • Balance corporate vs. individual messaging in hero copy.
03

Direction three

Friendly Student Academy

Warm and approachable without going childish. A course-finder-led hero, lively bento discovery, a visual student journey and a prominent human/WhatsApp contact path — for learners and parents who want reassurance, not a sales pitch.

English IELTS Business Kids & Youth
Hero wireframe · desktop

Desktop concept

An airy hero on a soft blue-to-cream wash with gentle floating shapes and rounded image frames. The headline leads straight into a course finder input plus popular-category chips, so discovery starts in the first screen. Below, a bento mix of differently-sized cards (never a uniform grid) and an illustrated student-journey path make the route from enquiry to enrolment feel human and clear.

Mobile concept

Friendly stacked hero with a full-width finder and big tappable category bubbles. Discovery and journey become swipeable. A warm, sticky "Talk to us / WhatsApp" bar foregrounds human help over hard selling.

Hero layout

Left: warm headline → course-finder bar (the primary action) → popular category chips. Right: rounded student image collage with soft floating shapes. Reassurance microcopy ("Not sure? Talk to admissions") sits just under the finder.

▼ Risks / avoid

  • Restrain pastels & roundness; protect corporate credibility.
  • Trust must still feel serious, not cute.
  • Mind carousel UX pitfalls (discoverability, accessibility).

Recommendation

Lead with Direction 1's energy, borrow Direction 2's proof.

The brief explicitly asks for lively — so Bold Energetic (01) is the strongest spine. But its one weakness (looking flashy to corporate buyers) is exactly Premium Corporate (02)'s strength. The recommended path is a hybrid: keep 01's motion hero, ticker and dark/light rhythm, but pin 02's accreditation bar directly under the hero and give corporate training a dedicated premium band. Pull in 03's course-finder only if discovery-led conversion tests well.

My suggestion: pick the direction (or greenlight the hybrid) and I'll build a single fully-interactive hi-fi homepage — real hover/scroll motion, working finder, and the live lead/WhatsApp flow wired in.
